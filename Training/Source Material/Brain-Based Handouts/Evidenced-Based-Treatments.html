Evidenced-Based Treatments and Sample Accommodations Based on Brain-Based Needs

Brain-Based Practice: From Understanding to Action

Parent Management Trainings (PMTs)

PMTs, such as Parent-Child Interaction Therapy (PCIT) and Triple P (Positive Parenting Program), are evidence-based treatments that focus on improving the parent-child relationship and managing children's behavioral issues.

If... Then...
If the child and/or caregiver have a Low IQ Adjust the treatment pace by slowing it down and reinforcing session content over multiple sessions
If the client's developmental age is younger than stated age Match treatment to developmental level. Make the steps very simple/concrete with lots of repetition

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT is a therapeutic approach that helps individuals identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors that contribute to emotional distress. By developing healthier thinking habits and coping strategies, CBT enables individuals to improve emotional regulation and manage challenges more effectively.

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

DBT is a type of therapy that helps individuals manage intense emotions and improve relationships by teaching skills like mindfulness, emotional regulation, and distress tolerance. It focuses on balancing acceptance with the need for change and was initially developed for borderline personality disorder.

If... Then...
If the client has memory issues or has difficulty translating information outside of session Slow down pace of treatment, focusing on one skill at a time for multiple sessions, include support people and have them practice daily with clients, video skills so they can review between sessions

Trust-Based Relational Intervention (TBRI)

TBRI is an attachment-based, trauma-informed therapy designed to help children who have experienced trauma or adversity. It focuses on building trust, enhancing emotional regulation, and improving relationships through principles of connection, empowerment, and correction.

If... Then...
If the child has working memory challenges and a highly concrete thinking style Use Life Value Terms by focusing on one rule at a time in a concrete manner, incorporating role play. Reinforce learning through repetition and frequent review of the rules in each session
