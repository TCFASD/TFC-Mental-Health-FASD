Brain-Based Pathway: Competencies Framework

A Developmental Roadmap for Trauma-Informed, Neurobehavioral Care

The Brain-Based Pathway provides clear benchmarks for professionals working with individuals with FASD and other brain-based differences. It maps the journey from foundational knowledge to advanced, dynamic practice through two competency levels and six core developmental stages.

This document uses color alongside text labels to distinguish competency levels. Green highlights indicate Brain-Based Informed content; blue/teal highlights indicate Brain-Based Responsive content. All information is also conveyed in text.

Two Levels of Practice

Brain-Based Informed Practice

Foundational understanding — beginning application

Brain-based informed practice represents the foundational level of understanding regarding (a) brain functioning and its impact on behavior, learning, and emotional regulation, and (b) the therapeutic process itself. Practitioners at this level have completed introductory training and have fundamental knowledge of prenatal alcohol exposure and its impact on brain functioning and behavior. They demonstrate understanding of the importance of applying a brain-based lens to their work; however, they remain in the preliminary stages of translating this theoretical knowledge into consistent, evidence-based practice applications.

Brain-Based Responsive Practice

Advanced integration — real-time adaptation

Brain-based responsive practice represents the advanced level of understanding regarding (a) brain functioning and its impact on behavior, learning, and emotional regulation, and (b) the therapeutic process itself. Practitioners at this level demonstrate the ability to think holistically about the whole person and consistently adapt their methods, environmental factors, and interactions in real-time. They maintain a strong foundation in brain-based approaches through evidence-based accommodations, ongoing caregiver collaboration, interdisciplinary consultation, and regular professional development.

Core Competency Areas

Foundational Knowledge

Understanding brain development, neuroplasticity, and how neurobiological processes influence behavior and learning across the lifespan
Brain-Based Informed
  • Basic understanding of brain development, function, and neuroplasticity across the lifespan
  • Recognition of how neurobiological processes influence behavior and learning
  • Awareness of transference and countertransference dynamics in therapeutic relationships, particularly related to executive functioning, sensory processing, and emotional regulation challenges
Brain-Based Responsive
  • Comprehensive understanding of brain development, function, and neuroplasticity with ability to apply across diverse presentations
  • Dynamic recognition and navigation of transference and countertransference in real-time

Trauma Integration

Recognizing how trauma alters brain functioning and integrating this understanding with neurodevelopmental differences
Brain-Based Informed
  • Recognize how trauma alters brain areas governing emotional regulation, memory, executive functioning, and stress response
  • Understand concepts like window of tolerance, unconscious awareness of safety or threat, and lasting changes in neural pathways
  • Recognize that cognitive deficits from prenatal alcohol exposure create barriers to processing trauma therapy
Brain-Based Responsive
  • Skilled implementation of interventions that account for permanent limitations by emphasizing environmental modifications, caregiver training, and concrete approaches
  • Adapt trauma interventions using neurobehavioral model with bottom-up approaches (focusing on body awareness and regulation before cognitive processing)

Prenatal Alcohol Exposure (PAE) Understanding

Deep knowledge of how prenatal alcohol exposure affects brain structure, function, and presentation
Brain-Based Informed
  • Recognition of nuanced and complex presentations within prenatal alcohol exposure
  • Understand how prenatal exposures affect brain structure and function
  • Understand the importance of screening for prenatal alcohol exposure across all ages, recognizing that a large number of individuals remain undiagnosed
  • Recognize organic brain differences that impact self-regulation, executive functioning, social cognition, memory, and learning
  • Create accommodations and include support systems in treatment
Brain-Based Responsive
  • Expertise in addressing brain differences across the neurodiversity spectrum, particularly when FASD is identified as a co-occurring condition
  • Skilled in nuanced FASD screening tailored to adoptive parents, birth parents, or adults, recognizing negative screens do not rule out FASD. Communicates sensitively, connects to resources, and supports referrals based on neurobiological understanding and co-occurring conditions
  • Skilled ability to implement individualized accommodations based on neurobiological understanding

Cause-Effect Reasoning

Understanding that brain damage affects abstract thinking and traditional consequences may be ineffective
Brain-Based Informed
  • Understand that individuals with PAE have impaired cause-and-effect reasoning due to brain damage affecting abstract thinking and working memory
  • Recognition that traditional behavior modification may be ineffective with delayed consequences
Brain-Based Responsive
  • Therapeutic interventions utilize immediate, concrete consequences and explicitly teach causal relationships
  • Real-time adaptation of approaches based on client's capacity for abstract reasoning

Client Education

Explaining symptoms and neurobiology in accessible, non-pathologizing ways that reduce shame
Brain-Based Informed
  • Ability to explain how symptoms relate to underlying brain processes in accessible, non-pathologizing ways
  • Help clients understand their neurobiology to reduce shame and increase self-compassion
Brain-Based Responsive
  • Develop effective coping strategies collaboratively that work with rather than against brain functioning patterns
  • Empower clients and support systems with actionable neurobiological understanding

Case Conceptualization

Integrating brain-based factors into assessment, diagnosis, and treatment planning
Brain-Based Informed
  • Recognition of brain-based factors in client presentation, case conceptualization, and treatment planning
Brain-Based Responsive
  • Holistic integration: Think comprehensively about co-morbid mental health and medical diagnoses associated with PAE
  • Dynamic treatment planning that evolves with client presentation

Professional Development

Ongoing learning and skill-building in brain-based approaches
Brain-Based Informed
  • Completion of relevant training and educational materials in brain-based approaches
Brain-Based Responsive
  • Application of knowledge acquired through advanced training, mentoring, consultation, and extensive practice experience
  • Ongoing interdisciplinary consultation and regular professional development

Advanced Responsive Competencies

The following competencies are exclusive to the Brain-Based Responsive level. They represent the hallmarks of advanced, integrated neurobehavioral practice and build upon all core competency areas above.

Real-Time Adaptation

The ability to recognize regulatory states and shift therapeutic approach dynamically during sessions
Brain-Based Responsive
  • Recognition of regulatory states and developmental capacity with ability to shift therapeutic approach dynamically during sessions
  • Observe early dysregulation indicators and immediately implement co-regulation strategies and sensory interventions
  • Modify session objectives and approach in real-time, prioritizing client's current neurobiological capacity

Intervention Modification

Adapting therapeutic techniques to match brain functioning patterns
Brain-Based Responsive
  • Incorporate movement or sensory strategies to support nervous system regulation
  • Adjust expectations and pacing based on executive functioning capacity
  • Maintain therapeutic alliance while adapting approaches

Reflective Practice

Continuous self-awareness about expectations, biases, and assumptions
Brain-Based Responsive
  • Continuous, active self-reflection of personal expectations and potential biases in treatment planning and service delivery
  • Acknowledge that expectations vary across clients and may shift over time, requiring ongoing awareness and modifications throughout treatment
