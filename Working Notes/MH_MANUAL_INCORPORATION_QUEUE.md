## 2026-05-16 — Progress notes, treatment plan timing, session cap

1. **Progress notes — avoid repetition.**
   Do not write repetitive notes. Each note must show distinct, ongoing
   therapeutic need. Repetitive notes undermine requests for additional units
   later — the documentation has to *prove* therapeutic need to justify more
   units.

2. **Treatment plan must be signed before treatment starts.**
   Best practice: do not begin treatment until the treatment plan is signed.

3. **Session cap: 48 sessions per fiscal year.**
   Fiscal year for this cap = January through December (calendar year).
   - TO CLARIFY before writing: the existing §3 guidance references a
     "July 1 reset" for Medicaid units. Confirm whether the 48-session
     Jan–Dec cap is a different payor/contract or supersedes the July 1
     rule, so the two don't conflict in the manual.

Routing (tentative):

- Item 2 → first session / treatment plan section
- Item 3 → §3 Billing (therapy unit limits)

## 2026-06-01 — Productivity & consultation tied to funding (§12.4) — APPLIED

Rewrote §12.4 Productivity Expectations to make productivity funding-based:

1. **75% billable / 25% admin.** Typically 75% of the job is billable to a
   funding source; the remaining 25% (~2 hrs/day) is administrative time.

2. **Consultation/case management tied to a funder, varies by program.** Some
   programs have a funding source linked specifically to consultation, case
   management, etc.; some do not. Where a funder pays for them, they count as
   billable (keep consultation small — ~1 hr/week). Where there is no funding
   connected to case management and consultation, those do not count as
   billable, so focus on direct mental health services.

This replaced the old §12.4 line "roughly 1½ hours of client work and 2 hours
of administrative work per day," which conflicted with OPS-112's 75% figure —
resolves [[manual-section-12-4-discrepancy]] in favor of OPS-112. Admin
(2 hrs/day) unchanged. Applied to manual paragraphs 385–386.
