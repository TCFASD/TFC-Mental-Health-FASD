How to Bill
The Billing Absolutes!

No ifs, ands, or buts...

Rule #1 - No client can be seen until consents are signed
Rule #2 - The assessment is always billed first - nothing else can be
billed before it (unless it's an ITXXCC or CCM which are not billable
codes)
Rule #3 - You must do a Treatment Plan within 10 business days from
the date of the assessment. TPs need to be signed by the caregiver
Rule #4 - You must track when documents expire and cannot see
clients until they have been renewed (this goes for consents,
assessments, treatment plans, and treatment plan reviews)

Individual session: If the therapist worked alone with the client

Family session with client present: If the therapist worked with the client and one or more family
members.

Family session without client present: If the therapist worked with one or more family members
and the client was not present (the session has to be pertinent to the client's therapeutic work)

Clinical Consultation (ITXXCC) and
Clinical Case Management (CCM)

Depending on funding, some therapists will have additional service codes:
ITXXCC (Clinical Consultation) - Use this when talking to someone via telephone; or when
talking to other support people (ie: consulting with the child's speech therapist or the counselor
at the child's school).
CCM (Clinical Case Management) - Use this when writing reports outside of your normal
documentation (ie: special reports for court, etc)

These codes can be used to document the therapist's time doing activities that cannot be billed
to insurance such as phone calls and writing reports for special circumstances (court reports).

Remember, you want to limit the amount of time you are talking to people outside of sessions.
You can politely say, "That sounds important. Let's set up time to talk." As long as this time is
in person or on Zoom - It's a billable session. If you end up talking by phone, then it's an
ITXXCC.
